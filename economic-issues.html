6. Economic issues

6.1 Attitudes toward lifting Western sanctions on Russia

Key Finding

78% of Ukrainians oppose lifting Western sanctions against Russia as a condition to end the war, with only 7% supporting such a move. This opposition has remained stable over the past year, reinforcing the brief's assertion that sanctions should remain until Russia takes viable steps to halt its aggression.

Source: Razumkov Center, March 2025
Source: Razumkov Center, 2024-2025

6.2 Implications for negotiation approaches

The strong societal consensus (78%) means sanctions relief should be linked to specific Russian actions rather than offered as a pre-emptive concession.

This position aligns with other non-negotiable components like captive returns and accountability for war crimes, reflecting Ukrainian insistence on maintaining pressure until tangible progress occurs.
